What is Cybersecurity?


Cybersecurity is the technology and process designed to protect networks and devices from attacks, damage, or unauthorized access. It is essential for a country’s military, hospitals, large corporations, small businesses, and other organizations, since data is now the cornerstone of everything. If that data is exploited, there can be many risks.


Some Quick Facts:


- A cyber attack happens roughly every 39 seconds on average.

- A large majority (around 95%) of data breaches are caused by human error, like weak passwords or clicking phishing links.

- Data breaches can be extremely costly for businesses, both in terms of financial loss and reputational damage.

- Attackers use malware like viruses and ransomware to infiltrate systems and steal data.

- Employees with malicious intent can pose a significant cyber security risk to their companies.

- Cybercrime is considered a lucrative business, with estimates of significant annual damages.


Vocabulary:


Network: A network is a connection between two or more computers that communicate.

Internet: The Internet connects a computer to any other computer anywhere in the world via dedicated routers and servers.

Internet Protocols: The set of rules that is followed to control the flow of the Internet.

IP Address: An Internet Protocol address is assigned to all devices that connect to a computer network and use the Internet Protocol for communication.

MAC Address: A unique identification number every device that connects to the internet has. Traditional MAC addresses are 12-digit hexadecimal numbers.

DNS: Consider the Domain Name Server as the phonebook of the internet. All the IP addresses and the names of the links are saved in it.

DHCP: The Dynamic Host Configuration Protocol assigns an IP address to any device that wants to connect to the Internet.

Router: This device routes the data that comes to it and then sends it to the destination to ensure that it is on the appropriate path.

Bots: Bots are computer programs that control your computer without your knowledge. They automatically send emails, retrieve web pages, and change computer settings.
